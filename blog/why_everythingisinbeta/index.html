I'm trying to keep track of several projects currently in beta using the Twitter hashtag #EverythingIsInBeta. It's more of an information point rather than something actually useful but, hey, it's Twitter. When was Twitter ever useful?

MY BRAIN HURTS!

But seriously, it's difficult to jump in and learn new technology. It makes it even more difficult when that technology is in beta. It becomes frustratingly dumbfounding when that beta technology is dependent upon other beta technologies. There are challenges here. More than once I've had to step away from demo projects due to various things not working. These non-working "things" are not necessarily bugs. They can simply be features that aren't complete or even my brain refusing to adapt to new ways of thinking.

Even TypeScript, the typed superset of Javascript that is used with Angular 2 is under development, though it seems to be fairly solid these days. Microsoft has published a TypeScript Roadmap if you'd like to see what is coming.

Finding Everything

You'll find #EverythingIsInBeta in my sidebar and [in this post]({{< relref "everythingisinbeta.md" >}}) with links to each project's Changelog.md. Well, except for the Ionic CLI project which never seems to have a changelog.

Keep in mind that, for instance, just because Angular 2 is at beta 15, it doesn't necessarily mean that Ionic 2 is using that beta. As a practical matter, the Ionic team is behind pace with the Angular 2 project to avoid Angular 2's bleeding edge. Once Angular 2 is released, I am certain that Ionic 2's release will follow quickly after.

Also, keep in mind, none of these projects are giving guidance as to when a release may be imminent. *"Soon"* is the only thing said by people close to these projects.

Anyway, you can keep up with the latest betas, follow me on Twitter and keep an eye out for #EverythingIsInBeta.
