COVID-19 Pandemic: Things for developers to do

May 1, 2020

Like everyone else, I have been affected by the COVID-19 Pandemic, and in ways I was not expecting. Sure, I've been working remotely from my home for a decade, so you would think that my mental/emotional state would not be affected. My brain seems to know that there is a difference between choosing to stay home and being told that you have to stay home (and if you don't you might get sick and die). My mental state took a nose dive in April, not long after Atlanta was ordered to Stay Home.

So, obviously, I needed to find things to keep myself from losing my mind. And I thought I'd share those with you here.

So, of course I was laid off

Yeah, just like 16 million Americans, my employer decided, right at the beginning of this madness that they needed to reduce staff. As far as I can tell, the decision had nothing to do with the pandemic, but a bunch of my co-workers and I were let go anyway. This isn't the first time I've been laid off, so I was better prepared for this than most. Even so, a layoff messes with everyone's head. Throw in a pandemic, and you've got all the mixings for depression.

I worked on my blog: I am a cliché

Having been stuck in my house without any work, I started to take a look at the thing I haven't been doing for myself in years: writing. So, as cliché as it sounds, I decided to work on my stale and lifeless blog. I had been using Hugo for my static site, but wasn't very satisfied by the workflow. Sure, the templates for Hugo look great, but the developer experience (at least three years ago) was not a lot of fun.

Having heard about Scully, the static site generator for Angular, and having seen Frosty himself talk about Scully on AngularAir, I thought I'd give it a go. I don't think I've gotten everything quite right yet, but expect a post about it when I do.

And, also expect more blog posts for me.

If you are an Angular developer you should subscribe to Angular Air. I enjoy their show.
